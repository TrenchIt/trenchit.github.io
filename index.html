Do you have galvanized water lines? Was your home built before 1990?

This may be surprising, but some contractors were still using galvanized water lines as late as 1990! Galvanized lines corrode and crack over time creating loss of water pressure, discoloration of drinking water, and pin hole sized leaks. If not corrected, leaks lead to mold and mildew in your home. The life expectancy of a galvanized water line is 50 years. These lines should be replaced before serious damage can occur.

Replace old or leaky pipes before it's too late!

Trench It specializes in replacing old and damaged pipes with modern materials that will last a lifetime. Preemptive replacement could save you thousands of dollars in potential damages.
